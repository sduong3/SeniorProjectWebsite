FPGA MIDI Synthesizer

About Crescendo


Our team created a MIDI synthesizer which accepts data through either wired or a wireless connection. This data is sent to a decoder which sorts the input bytes, determine the definition of the bytes and proceeds to change the state of the FPGA. This is written to memory. The synthesizer reads this data and processes it in preparation to the digital to analog converter. This DAC turns the digital data into an analog signal. After this process, the resulting waves are outputted as music.

Our Features


  • Wireless Bluetooth Interface through BlueSMiRF Modem
  • Wired input through 5 pin DIN connector and 61 key, velocity sensitive MIDI keyboard
  • Spartan 6 FPGA for sound synthesis
  • 3.5mm aux cable compatible

    • HDL Design

    The MIDI synthesizer uses two receivers, one decoder, a LUT and a sigma delta DAC to produce a bitstream. This can be turned into a sound wave based on serial input. A combination of pipelining and clock multiplication achieved accurate sound replication with an audio resolution of 13 bits.

    Hardware Design

    There are three main components: the first component is connected to the FPGA. The second serial input, DIN connector, is connected to an octocoupler to manage different grounds. Two low-pass filters lead to the 3.5mm headphone jack with a corner frequency of 28.219 kHZ.
